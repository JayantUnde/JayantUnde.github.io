About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute of Innovation for Future Society, Nagoya University, Japan, working in the Intelligent Robotics and Biomechatronics Laboratory. My research focuses on designing intelligent robotic systems that enhance human capabilities and improve quality of life. I specialize in assistive technologies, safe physical human-robot interaction, and robotic manipulation of clothing, with a particular emphasis on dressing assistance and the development of novel mechanisms such as high extension ratio linear actuators.

During my graduate studies, I developed robotic solutions for elderly care, including a high-extension mechanism for sit-to-stand assistance, a dressing system capable of handling human-worn garments, and end effectors designed for fabric separation.

I'm driven by a passion for robotics, mechanical design, and automation to solve real-world problems and foster inclusivity through intelligent assistive systems. I welcome collaboration with like-minded researchers and professionals. Feel free to connect via email or social links on this site.
