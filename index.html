About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute of Innovation for Future Society, Nagoya University, Japan, working in the Intelligent Robotics and Biomechatronics Laboratory. My research focuses on designing intelligent robotic systems to assist the elderly with their activities of daily living. I specialize in assistive robotics hardware development, particularly hardware with embodied intelligence for safe physical human-robot interaction for the complex challenge of robotic clothing manipulation.

My academic journey began with a B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering from COEP Technological University, followed by a Master’s and Ph.D. in Mechanical Systems Engineering and Micro-Nano Mechanical Science and Engineering at Nagoya University. During my graduate studies, I developed robotic solutions for elderly care, including high-extension actuators and end-effectors capable of handling human-worn garments and fabric separation.

I'm driven by a passion for robotics, mechanical design, and automation to solve real-world problems and foster inclusivity through intelligent assistive systems. I welcome collaboration with like-minded researchers and professionals. Feel free to connect via email or social links on this site.
